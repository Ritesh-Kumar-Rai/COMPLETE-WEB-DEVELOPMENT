What are Semantic Elements?

A semantic element clearly describes its meaning to both the browser and the developer.

Examples of non-semantic elements: <div> and <span> - Tells nothing about its content.
Examples of semantic elements: <form>, <table>, and <article> - Clearly defines its content.

Non-Semantic vs Semantic Tags

Semantic Tags Non-Semantic Tags
* Meaningful: Describe content. Generic: No specific meaning.
* SEO: Good for search engines. No SEO: Not SEO-friendly.
* Accessibility: Useful for screen readers. For Styling: Used for layout.
* Examples: <header>, <footer>, <article>, <section>, <nav> . Examples: <div>, <span>, <i>, <b>.
